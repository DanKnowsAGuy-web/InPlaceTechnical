Section 1

Product Overview

In-Place BaseGrade is a soil and base stabilization treatment that converts native soil and reclaimed road material into engineered structural base using cold, in-place application with standard construction equipment. The active chemistry is designated SS3MB — Sta'bl SOIL 3MB, where 3MB stands for Modified Macro Molecular Binder.

The product has been in commercial application for approximately two decades under several successive trade designations — PMB, SSPMB, 3MB, and Sta'bl SOIL — all referring to the same formulation. The third-party testing laboratory with the longest continuous relationship with the material has confirmed in writing that SS3MB is the same product previously tested under those earlier names. The full historical test record is continuous and traceable.

In-Place BaseGrade is distributed exclusively in North America by Build Plus LLC.

Material characteristics: Colorless liquid, pH 7–8, specific gravity 1.05. Non-flammable. Not regulated as a hazardous material by US DOT, IATA, IMDG, or ADR. NFPA: Health 1, Flammability 0, Reactivity 0. Non-toxic, VOC-free, 90% biodegradable by OECD 302B. Cleanup with water.

Section 2

Mechanism of Action

When the diluted binder is blended uniformly into soil at controlled moisture content and the mixture is compacted, the binder forms a crosslinked molecular structure through the soil matrix. It coats and bonds fine particles to larger aggregate, fills voids, and consolidates the mass into a semi-flexible, load-bearing layer. Strength derives from the binder network working in combination with mechanical compaction.

Semi-flexible behavior. Unlike cement-stabilized base, which is rigid and prone to shrinkage cracking, the treated material retains slight flexibility under traffic loading and thermal cycling — measurable via resilient modulus and indirect tensile testing (Section 5).

Section 5

Site Selection & Application

Candidate Road Profile

In-Place BaseGrade is designed for full-depth reclamation (FDR) and granular base stabilization (GBS). A well-suited candidate shows distressed bituminous surface over granular base — fatigue cracking, block cracking, or potholes — with a granular surface that does not rut easily when wet. Full-depth distress not driven by subgrade failure. Existing or correctable drainage. Minimum 6 inches of reclaimable material; maximum reclamation depth 10 inches.

Application Process

  1. Native soil samples are taken and tested to establish composition and binder dosage. Mix design is soil-specific and is completed for every project before work begins.
  2. Concentrate is diluted with potable water to the specified ratio and blended into the soil with a reclaimer or milling machine at 6–12 inches depth, targeting 8–13% moisture content.
  3. The blended material is graded and compacted to maximum achievable density. Compaction of 98% or higher is consistently achieved.
  4. The layer cures by natural drying to the project-specified strength targets.
  5. A moisture-sealing surface treatment is applied, followed by the specified wear surface — chip seal, microsurfacing, or hot-mix asphalt overlay.

Section 7

Standard Next-Step Testing Protocol

The following sequence is standard practice for bringing any new stabilization material into an agency's approved-products list or structural pavement design. Build Plus provides project mix design support and can facilitate third-party testing coordination for each item.

1
Resilient Modulus and Structural Layer Coefficient
Resilient modulus testing on project-representative specimens, with derivation of the structural layer coefficient (a-value) for AASHTO 1993 or mechanistic-empirical pavement thickness design — the standard entry point for writing any stabilized material into a pavement section.
2
Moisture Durability and Freeze-Thaw Cycling
Retained-strength testing after moisture conditioning (ASTM D4867) and wet-dry / freeze-thaw durability testing (ASTM D559/D560 family). Standard protocol for stabilized base entering service in wet or freeze-prone climates.
3
Unconfined Compressive Strength on Project Mix Design
UCS testing (ASTM D1633 protocol) on the project-specific mix design — binder-alone and, where applicable, binder-plus-cement — to establish design strength values tied to the native material.
4
Project Mix Design on Native Soils
A full engineered-binder mix design against the five performance properties using a minimum 350-pound sample of the project's native material. Included as standard with all Build Plus-supplied projects.
5
Current Third-Party Accreditation
For agencies requiring testing under current AASHTO accreditation, Build Plus can coordinate with a qualified laboratory. Contact us to arrange.
6
Environmental Review
For projects near groundwater or surface water, project-specific leachate and aquatic-toxicity review against jurisdiction requirements. Full SDS available on request. The product is non-toxic, non-VOC, non-regulated by US DOT, and 90% biodegradable by OECD 302B.

Summary

For the Non-Technical Reader

In-Place BaseGrade is a water-based liquid mixed into existing road material or native soil and compacted in place. It binds the soil particles together and converts marginal native soil into structural road base — eliminating or substantially reducing the cost of hauling aggregate from off-site. Independent laboratory tests show large strength improvements, including nearly doubling the load-bearing strength of difficult clay soils. The material gains most of its strength within a few days of placement. It is non-toxic, applied with no heat, and works with the same equipment used in standard road construction.

It requires a wearing layer on top — chip seal, microsurfacing, or asphalt overlay. It is designed for roads with base and surface distress, not for roads whose primary failure is in the subgrade. A pre-design soil evaluation is standard practice and is included with all Build Plus-supplied projects.

Consistent, well-documented strength improvement across soil types in independent laboratory testing. A proven application process with a full performance specification framework. Successful construction across climates from the arid Southwest to the Midwest freeze-thaw cycle.

Appendix

Glossary of Terms

AASHTO
American Association of State Highway and Transportation Officials. Publishes pavement design standards and materials test methods.
ASTM
ASTM International. Publishes standardized test methods referenced throughout this document.
CBR — California Bearing Ratio
Load-bearing strength of soil expressed as a percentage of a crushed-stone reference. Higher is stronger; a primary input for pavement thickness design.
DCP — Dynamic Cone Penetrometer
A probe driven into a layer to estimate in-place strength by depth.
ESAL — Equivalent Single Axle Load
Standard unit for counting traffic damage as equivalent passes of an 18,000-pound axle.
FDR — Full Depth Reclamation
Rebuilding a road by grinding the existing surface and base and treating the reclaimed material in place.
FWD — Falling Weight Deflectometer
Drops a known load and measures deflection to back-calculate layer stiffness values.
GBS — Granular Base Stabilization
Stabilization of an existing granular base layer, typically without full reclamation.
ITS / IDT — Indirect Tensile Strength
Measures resistance to being pulled apart. Relates to cracking resistance under thermal cycling and traffic.
P200
Percentage of material passing the No. 200 sieve — the very fine fraction. Higher P200 generally indicates more moisture-sensitive material.
PI — Plasticity Index
The moisture range over which a clay behaves plastically. Lower PI means a more stable, less swell-prone soil.
RAP — Recycled Asphalt Pavement
Ground-up material from an existing asphalt road, reused as base material in FDR applications.
Resilient Modulus
Stiffness of a material under repeated traffic loading. The primary structural input for mechanistic pavement design.
Structural Layer Coefficient
The a-value in AASHTO design expressing how much structural capacity a unit thickness of a layer contributes.
UCS — Unconfined Compressive Strength
Crushing strength of a cylindrical specimen with no lateral support. Standard strength metric for stabilized materials.
VOC — Volatile Organic Compound
Evaporating organic chemical. SS3MB is VOC-free.
Performance values are attributed to their sources throughout. Independent laboratory data, third-party field documentation, and manufacturer technical literature are each identified in context. Project-specific performance is dependent on native soil composition, site conditions, and mix design. A pre-design soil evaluation is standard with all Build Plus-supplied projects.
